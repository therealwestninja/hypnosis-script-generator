# Upgrades for a browser-native hypnosis tool

### A two-tier voice architecture is the strategic upgrade

Offer a "Premium Voice" toggle that lazy-downloads **Kokoro-82M at q4f16 quantization (~40 MB)** and caches it via the Cache API. Kokoro is MIT/Apache-licensed, tops the TTS Arena among free open models, runs via Transformers.js with WebGPU acceleration on Chrome/Edge (WASM fallback elsewhere), and supports streaming output. Piper's low-quality voices (~15 MB including ONNX runtime and espeak-ng phonemizer) are the smaller alternative. The transformative move: **pre-generate the entire script to an AudioBuffer or Blob before playback**, then play it through an `<audio>` element with `navigator.mediaSession` metadata. This single architecture choice simultaneously solves the 15-second bug, the iOS lock-screen bug, `onend` unreliability, ducking (since the audio is now in your Web Audio graph), and makes the audio-recording export feature trivial. The W3C Web Speech CG has the synthesis redesign on its 2026 roadmap but nothing ships yet; do not design around a hypothetical v2.

## Features worth stealing from Calm, Reveri, HypnoBox and friends

The competitive landscape clusters into three archetypes. **Reveri** (Stanford's Dr. David Spiegel) differentiates through *real-time adaptive sessions* — a 30-second interactive Q&A shapes a 10-minute personalized induction, and its clinically-validated **Hypnotic Induction Profile** sorts users into three hypnotizability tiers to personalize recommendations. **HypnoBox** is the modular-customization extreme: 600+ suggestion modules the user combines with induction→deepening→suggestion→awakening scaffolding, a distinctive "Double Induction" with male+female voices simultaneously, and an "Active Alert" mode for use during cardio. **Balance** pioneered dynamic session assembly — a library of thousands of audio clips stitched together based on an onboarding quiz plus daily pre-session questions.

Three patterns recur across Calm, Headspace, Insight Timer, Waking Up, Medito, Oak, and Smiling Mind that transfer directly to a browser tool. The first is **the 10-minute standard length** with a "Quick Relief" 1–2 minute micro-dose option for momentary state change. The second is **modular audio production** — Glenn Harrold's signature technique of stereo-echoed affirmations panning ear-to-ear, binaural beats tuned to specific musical keys, and post-hypnotic affirmations delivered at the deepest point. The third is **ambient-plus-vocal mixing** à la Omvana, where users combine any vocal track over any ambient bed for 60,000+ combinations — a feature space your tool can beat by synthesizing the ambience procedurally.

### Browser APIs that unlock competitor-class features

**Screen Wake Lock** (`navigator.wakeLock.request('screen')`) reached universal support in 2024 — Chrome 85+, Edge 90+, Firefox 124+, Safari 16.6+ desktop and iOS, all Android. Re-acquire on `visibilitychange` since it auto-releases when hidden. Critical for sessions longer than the device's screen timeout. **Vibration API** (`navigator.vibrate`) is supported in Chrome/Firefox/Edge/Samsung/Opera but *not* Safari/iOS; patterns are capped at 10 entries and 10 s each. A breathing haptic of `[400, 3600, 400, 7600]` ms (inhale-pulse, hold, exhale-pulse, hold) approximates 4-7-8 breathing eyes-closed. Use for session-start/end markers and phase transitions, never for rhythmic strobing.

## Synthesize every sound instead of shipping samples

A fully procedural audio engine is not only possible in pure Web Audio — it is competitive with sample-based meditation apps because it never loops audibly and can cross-fade between scenes smoothly. The master chain is always `source → sceneGain → masterGain → DynamicsCompressor → destination`, with compressor settings `threshold −18, knee 24, ratio 3, attack 0.01, release 0.25` to prevent clipping and polish the sum. **Never change gains abruptly** — always `linearRampToValueAtTime` over ≥10 ms, and use 0.0001 as a floor for `exponentialRampToValueAtTime` since it can't touch zero.

**Binaural beats** are two pure sines at `carrier ± beat/2` routed through a `ChannelMergerNode` (not `StereoPannerNode`, because panners may add crosstalk that attenuates the neural effect). Carriers of 200 Hz work well; the standard brainwave map is Delta 0.5–4 Hz (deep sleep), Theta 4–8 Hz (trance), Alpha 8–13 Hz (relaxation), Beta 13–30 Hz (focus), Gamma 40 Hz (insight). Gate the feature behind a "headphones confirmed" checkbox — through speakers the two tones sum acoustically into ordinary AM beating, defeating the premise. Note honestly in the UI that binaural-beat efficacy is **contested in peer-reviewed literature** (Park 2018 and García-Argibay 2017 found effects; López-Caballero 2017 and Goodin 2012 did not).

**Isochronic tones** work on speakers — a single tone gated on/off at the target frequency. The click-free envelope requires an exponential ramp attack/release of ~30–50 ms of the pulse period, scheduled in 20–30-second batches inside a lookahead scheduler so the automation queue doesn't grow unbounded.

**Noise is the substrate for every soundscape.** White noise is a 2-second AudioBuffer filled with `Math.random()*2-1`, looped; anything shorter and the ear locks onto the repetition. Pink noise uses **Paul Kellet's 7-coefficient IIR filter** (firstpr.com.au), accurate to ±0.05 dB above 9 Hz, best implemented in an `AudioWorkletProcessor`. Brown noise is a leaky-integrated white sum (`last = (last + 0.02*w) / 1.02`). These three feed everything else.

### Natural soundscape recipes

**Rain** is a layered trick, not a single process: a pink-noise bandpass bed at 1–2 kHz with a slow gain LFO, a brown-noise rumble 20 dB below at lowpass 200 Hz, and — crucially — **scheduled droplet transients**, 5–20-ms bandpass-filtered white bursts following a Poisson process at 20–50 ms mean inter-arrival, each with randomized center frequency 2–6 kHz. Without the droplets, rain sounds like static. **Ocean waves** are pink or brown noise bandpassed 400–900 Hz with gain modulated by a very slow sine LFO at 1/11 Hz (11-second wave period), with stereo decorrelation via a 10–20 ms Haas delay on one channel. **Wind** is filtered noise with the bandpass frequency itself modulated by a random LFO. **Crackling fire** combines a brown-noise bed at −25 dB with sharp 3–10 ms bandpass-filtered "pops" at random intervals. **Singing bowls** are additive synthesis of inharmonic partials at ratios roughly 1 : 2.02 : 3.07 : 4.16 : 5.38 : 6.71 with long exponential decays (10, 7, 5, 4, 3, 2.5 seconds), plus a 10-ms noise strike transient at onset for realism. **Om drones** stack detuned sines at root/fifth/octave with peaking filters at vocal formants 500 Hz and 1500 Hz for vowel color, plus ±8-cent detune LFOs for breath. **Heartbeat** is a 1.0–1.17 s-period "lub-dub" of two low sine thumps 140 ms apart through a 200 Hz lowpass. **Forest/birds** is the honest hard case — realistic bird chirps require per-species FM patches, so document that forest in a no-sample tool is a stylized rustling-leaves bed with occasional algorithmic whistles, not realistic audio.

**Synthetic reverb without an IR file** uses a decaying-noise ConvolverNode buffer (the same trick Tone.js's `Reverb` uses): fill a 3-4-second stereo AudioBuffer with `(random()*2-1) * pow(1-i/len, decay)`, assign to `convolver.buffer`, done. For mobile, prefer a cheaper Schroeder-style network — four parallel DelayNode comb filters (29/37/41/43 ms with feedback gains ≈0.78 and lowpass in the feedback path) feeding two series allpass-style stages. Either approach runs continuously at trivial CPU.

**Ducking narration against ambience** must be triggered from `utterance.onstart` (ramp ambience to 0.2 over 400 ms) and `utterance.onend`/`onerror` (ramp back to 1.0 over 800 ms), because SpeechSynthesis output does not traverse the Web Audio graph — the OS renders it separately. Target levels: narration peaks ~ −3 dBFS, ambience −15 to −20 dB during speech, −6 to −10 dB between utterances. This ducking logic is another reason to consider the WASM-TTS tier: with Kokoro, narration *is* in the graph and true sidechain compression becomes possible.

**AudioContext autoplay policy** means the context starts suspended; call `ctx.resume()` inside the first user gesture on your "Begin session" button. iOS Safari specifically needs a `touchend` event, not `touchstart`, and may still silence output unless a silent `<audio>` element is playing alongside — a known workaround.

## Fifteen hypnotic visuals beyond spiral and pendulum

**Safety is the design constraint.** WCAG 2.3.1 permits at most three opposing luminance transitions per second, with the danger zone 3–30 Hz and saturated red being especially hazardous. Cap all brightness/hue oscillations at ≤2 Hz, never use saturated red with opposing transitions, avoid high-contrast stripe patterns wider than 25% of view, and always respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Hypnotic effects want slow movement anyway, so the constraint largely aligns with good design.

The **breathing circle** is the single most evidence-based effect — a circle whose radius follows a piecewise-eased envelope over the breath cycle (4-7-8, box breathing 4-4-4-4, or coherent 5-5), with 3–5 concentric rings at phase offsets, color-shifting between cool blue inhale and warm amber exhale, and synchronized text labels cross-fading at transitions. Implement with a soft trail (`fillRect` alpha 0.10–0.20) rather than a full clear.

**Flow-field particles** driven by 3D simplex noise (`θ = noise3D(x·s, y·s, t·0.0005) · TAU`) with 500–1500 particles on desktop, 200–400 on mobile, drawn as short line segments from previous to current position with `globalCompositeOperation='lighter'` for additive glow — this is Daniel Shiffman's canonical flow-field and reads as organic, coherent "wind." Add **gravity-well attractors** at the center (inverse-square pull plus a small tangential component to induce orbits) for hypnotic focal lock. **Constellation lines** between nearby particles need a spatial hash grid with bucket size = threshold distance to stay O(n) rather than O(n²).

**Moiré patterns** are historically the most potent trance device and are simple: draw two identical line-grating or concentric-ring patterns, rotate the second by 1–3° and translate it slowly (5–15 px/s), and the interference produces massive phantom low-frequency bands. Circular moiré with one center offset orbiting the other is especially effective. Use medium-grays (#888/#000) not pure black-white to soften contrast and stay within WCAG.

**Water ripples** are concentric circles expanding from random sources with alpha falling quadratically as radius grows, spawned every 900–1600 ms, drawn with `lighter` compositing so overlapping ripples add brightness. **Doom-fire** (Fabien Sanglard's algorithm) runs trivially at 160×100 resolution — a 37-entry black→red→yellow→white palette, upward cellular diffusion with random jitter — then `drawImage` scaled up pixelated. Move it to a Worker via `transferControlToOffscreen` for guaranteed main-thread headroom. **Candle focal** is a single soft radial-gradient glow with 3-pixel position jitter and ±15% radius modulation — the classic Mesmerist's candle, potent alone.

**Starfield warp tunnel** projects 3D stars to 2D via `sx = x/z*focal + cx`; decrement z each frame, respawn when z<1. For warp streaks, draw a line from the previous projection to the current one — length grows with speed. Use `fillRect` for tiny star points (4× faster than `arc`+`fill`). **Classic plasma** (sum of four sines plus a radial term mapped through an HSV palette) belongs in an `ImageData` buffer at 256×192 with a 1024-entry sine LUT, then scaled via `drawImage`. **Kaleidoscope** is a meta-effect: wrap any other effect with N-fold mirror symmetry by clipping triangular wedges and mirroring alternate segments with `scale(-1,1)` to hide seams.

**Harmonograph curves** (sum of damped sines for x and y) with sub-stepped path-drawing and 0.01-alpha trail fade produce endless precessing Lissajous loops when frequencies are near-integer ratios. **Color breathing** is the minimum-motion mode — a single full-screen radial or conic gradient with slowly drifting HSL hue at 0.01–0.02 Hz — the gold standard for photosensitive users. **Droste feedback** uses `ctx.drawImage(canvas, ...)` with a tiny rotate + 1.012× scale per frame, creating an infinite-tunnel zoom from whatever is drawn at center. Draw a faint full-screen black rgba(0,0,0,0.01) every ~300 frames to prevent color saturation accumulation.

**Typewriter-revealed affirmations** (`substring(0, floor(t*charsPerSec))` at 60–90 ms per character) synchronized to the breath cycle — display during the hold phase for 2 seconds — dim the underlying effect 30% while text is on so they don't compete. Pair all of these with an L-system fractal tree for the awakening phase.

Skip autostereograms/Magic Eye entirely — they require open eyes and active fusion, the opposite of hypnotic relaxation. The right composition rule is "only one high-motion effect on screen at once, always with a slow breath pacer visible for entrainment." A sensible stack by session phase: induction uses breathing-circle over color-breathing, deepening uses water-ripples or low-speed starfield, core trance uses moiré or kaleidoscope-wrapped flow-field, affirmation uses candle focal plus typewriter text, emergence uses tree growth or sunrise gradient.

### Performance rules for 60fps over 30 minutes

Canvas 2D contexts should be created with `{alpha: false, desynchronized: true}` when filling the background. Use faded `fillRect` instead of `clearRect`. Pre-render static layers (moiré grating base, mandala skeleton) to OffscreenCanvas once and blit with `drawImage`. Move heavy pixel work (Doom-fire, plasma, water cellular automata) to a Worker via `transferControlToOffscreen` — Baseline since Safari 16.4. Always use `requestAnimationFrame` with clamped delta time (`Math.min(50, now-last)`) to survive tab switches, and pause the rAF loop on `document.hidden` to save battery. Clamp device-pixel-ratio to 1.5 on mobile — full 3× retina kills fill rate. Use **object pools** for particles and ripples, mutating in place rather than allocating — GC pauses are felt in 30-minute sessions. Precompute sine LUTs for plasma and flow-fields. Never `getImageData` from the visible canvas per frame; render into an `ImageData`-backed offscreen and `drawImage` over.

## Accessibility is a content multiplier, not a checklist

The wizard needs one persistent `aria-live="polite"` region declared on load (screen readers miss updates to regions created dynamically), updated on each step change with "Step 3 of 5: Choose intention," plus DOM focus moved to the step's `<h2>` with `tabindex="-1"` so users reorient naturally. Add `aria-current="step"` on the active item in a visible progress list. Mark every canvas, decorative SVG, and ambient background element `aria-hidden="true"`. Give every icon-only button an `aria-label` and toggles an `aria-pressed`.

**Reduced motion** needs real alternatives, not just `animation: none`. Detect via both `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)` and `matchMedia('(prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)')` in JS, and swap the visual to a static focal point with slow opacity pulse (≥4 s period) or color-breathing mode. Offer a user override with three levels (Off / Gentle / Full) since some users want more motion than their OS default. Cap flicker at 2 Hz universally, ship a "no flicker" toggle regardless.

**Contrast and color modes.** Dark mode as default for hypnosis (low light reduces arousal). Support `prefers-contrast: more` and `forced-colors: active` by swapping to CSS System Colors (`Canvas`, `CanvasText`, `Highlight`) inside that media query rather than fighting the user's palette. Target **AAA 7:1** for the script text specifically — users read it with eyes half-closed. For the 24 guide personas and 5 categories, use the **Okabe–Ito 8-color colorblind-safe palette** and pair every color with an icon/label — never color alone.

**Cognitive-safety and trauma considerations are non-optional for hypnosis specifically.** Show content warnings before regression or deep-trance sessions and require explicit confirmation. Never auto-launch from a shared URL — display the config first and require the user to press Start (also WCAG 2.2.2 compliant). Ship a built-in **grounding exercise** (5-4-3-2-1 senses or paced breathing) reachable with one click from any session state, and route there automatically after an emergency end rather than dumping the user on a blank screen. Avoid default imagery that triggers common phobias: no closing walls (claustrophobia), no falling loops (vertigo), no rising water, no tunnels by default — offer neutral alternatives (open sky, candle, color field). Consider a **"safe word" text input** that immediately ends the session when typed.


### What not to build

Keyboard shortcuts. Undo/Redo. SSML as input, skip — preprocess your own pseudo-SSML instead. A single long utterance, never. Silent-utterance pauses, unreliable — use `setTimeout`. Saturated-red or >2 Hz flicker in any visual, ever. Auto-play from shared links, violates WCAG. Binaural beats presented as settled science — the peer-reviewed evidence is genuinely mixed and honest framing builds trust. A JS focus trap inside native `<dialog>` — the browser handles inertness correctly.
